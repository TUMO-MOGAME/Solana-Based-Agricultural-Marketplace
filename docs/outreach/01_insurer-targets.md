# Insurer targets

Four licensed South African short-term insurers with an agriculture line. Approach in this order — the recommended sequence is based on fit, openness to innovation, and scale of agri portfolio.

| # | Insurer | Why on the list | Angle to lead with |
|-|-|-|-|
| 1 | **Land Bank Insurance Company (LBIC)** | Government-aligned. Purpose-built for South African agriculture. Smallholder mandate sits squarely inside their remit. | "Public-development alignment. We help your existing smallholder mandate reach further at lower cost." |
| 2 | **Santam Agriculture** | Largest agri short-term insurer in SA. Already underwrite parametric products. Mature actuarial team. | "You already do parametric. We bring you a digital distribution channel into the smallholder market you can't economically serve through brokers." |
| 3 | **Hollard Insure (Agri)** | Has a record of fintech / partnership-driven products (Yalu, Naked etc. relationships). More likely to entertain a startup. | "You have a track record of partnering with smaller innovators. We are looking for an underwriting partner for a smallholder parametric maize product." |
| 4 | **Old Mutual Insure (OMI)** | Major SA insurer with agricultural lines. Inclusive-insurance interest. | "Inclusive insurance for smallholders. We provide the digital distribution; you bring the underwriting and balance sheet." |

## What we are asking for in the first call

A 30-minute introductory conversation to:
1. Understand their appetite for parametric, smallholder-scale crop insurance.
2. Ask what their minimum viable partnership would look like.
3. Find out who internally we should talk to for a real proposal (product head, agri underwriter, partnerships team).

**We are NOT asking for:** a quote, a binding commitment, a term sheet, or product approval.

## What to leave them with

- The one-pager PDF (attached to the cold email)
- Our contact details
- A clear next step we propose (e.g. "Could we share a draft term sheet for a structured-product partnership in 2-3 weeks?")

## Researching contacts

We need a real person at each insurer, not a generic info@ inbox. Sources:

- **LinkedIn** — search "[Insurer name] agriculture" or "[Insurer name] underwriter" — filter by location South Africa.
- Their **own websites** under "Agriculture" / "Business" / "Specialty" — head-of-product is often named.
- **Industry directories** — South African Insurance Association (SAIA), Insurance Institute of South Africa (IISA).
- **News articles** about each insurer's agri division — the journalist often quotes the head of agri.
- **University of Pretoria, Stellenbosch** ag-finance research papers — co-authors at insurers are often the right person.

Start at the **product / specialty / agri-line head** level. Avoid "info@" and avoid the CEO. The product head is the right altitude.
