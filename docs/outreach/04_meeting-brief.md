# Meeting brief

> Read this before any insurer call. Print or have it open in a second window. The call is 30 minutes — every minute matters.

## Goals for the call

In priority order:

1. **Find out if they would entertain underwriting** a smallholder parametric maize product through a digital partner.
2. **Identify the internal owner** of that decision (who, what role, what's their email).
3. **Learn the minimum viable shape** of a partnership — pricing model, capital requirements, regulatory cover, branding, data sharing.
4. **Understand their red lines** — what they will not do.
5. **Agree a concrete next step** before we hang up.

We are NOT trying to close anything. We are trying to learn the shape of the deal.

## Roles in the call

- **Tumo:** lead the call solo. Open, frame the problem, ask the questions, close.
- Record the call (with explicit permission) so you can review it afterwards — solo callers miss things in real time.
- Take written notes during the call too, but rely on the recording for the detail.

## Opening (the first 90 seconds)

> "Thank you for the time. I will keep this to 30 minutes. Let me give you 2 minutes of context on Vuna, then I have five questions I would value your view on, and we can take it from there."

Then in 2 minutes, cover:
1. Who you are (one sentence).
2. The problem (smallholders + financing gap + insurance penetration < 3%).
3. The product (Grow Pack: credit + inputs + parametric drought cover, mobile, co-op-mediated).
4. Where Vuna stops and where they start (we don't underwrite; we are looking for a licensed partner).
5. What you want from this call (their view on whether and how this could work).

**Don't pitch beyond 2 minutes.** They will switch off.

## The five questions to ask

1. **"Has [Insurer] underwritten parametric crop products before in South Africa? At what scale?"**
   *Why ask:* tells you whether you are educating them or learning from them.

2. **"For a smallholder maize parametric product, what would your minimum viable cohort size and pool be — and what is the pricing model you would expect?"**
   *Why ask:* surfaces the unit economics. Listen for: per-policy minimum, reinsurance threshold, premium-to-cover ratio.

3. **"Who at [Insurer] would own a partnership of this kind — product, agriculture line, partnerships, or strategic ventures?"**
   *Why ask:* identifies the decision-maker. Get a name and an email.

4. **"What data would you need from us to underwrite confidently — historical rainfall, satellite vegetation indices, harvest yields, default rates?"**
   *Why ask:* tells you what we have to build before we can come back with a real proposal.

5. **"If we wanted to run a 50-100 farmer pilot in the next planting season, what is the fastest realistic path inside your organisation — and what are the deal-killers we should know about now?"**
   *Why ask:* a concrete, scoped ask. Surfaces internal friction without committing either side.

## What to listen for (red and green flags)

| Green flags | Red flags |
|-|-|
| "We've done parametric before" | "Parametric? I'd need to look that up." |
| "Reinsurance is something we can structure" | "We don't take new lines without three years of loss data." |
| Specific named contacts | "Send it to info@..." |
| "Let me bring in our actuary" | "We'll get back to you" with no date |
| Curious questions about co-op model | Only questions about commercial terms |

## What to commit to in the call

Only commit to one of:
- **Send a more detailed product brief** within 7 working days
- **A second meeting** with their named internal contact within 14 working days
- **A pilot scoping document** within 21 working days

Never agree to:
- Exclusivity (we are talking to multiple insurers)
- Pricing
- Pilot dates without their internal stakeholder confirmed
- Signing anything in the first call

## Closing the call (the last 90 seconds)

1. Summarise what you heard in two sentences.
2. Confirm the agreed next step and date.
3. Ask: "Is there anyone else on your side I should be talking to?"
4. Thank them. End on time.

## After the call (within 24 hours)

1. Send `05_followup-template.md` with the agreed next step.
2. Update the contact log in `01_insurer-targets.md`.
3. Internal debrief: did you get the five answers? What surprised you? What would you do differently next call?
